### About

I am an R&D Engineer in the Department of Scientific Intelligence and Computing at the **National Institute of Clean-and-Low-Carbon Energy (NICE)** focusing on **AI for Science**. Before joining NICE, I was an assistant professor in the **Department of Computer Sciences and Artificial Intelligence (School of Information Engineering)** at the **China University of Geosciences (Beijing)**, where I taught computer vision and data science courses and mentored students in artificial intelligence, software engineering, and big data majors.

I have hybrid backgrounds in geoscience and computer science. I have participanted in the [Deep-time Digital Earth (DDE)](https://www.ddeworld.org/) and [EarthCube](https://www.earthcube.org/) projects. I am a co-founder of the [GISphere Corporation](https://gisphere.info/).

I obtained my Ph.D. degree at **University of Wisconsin-Madison** where I was co-advised by [Shaun Marcott](https://geoscience.wisc.edu/people/marcott-shaun/) and [Shanan Peters](http://strata.geology.wisc.edu/) with a focus on quantitative/data-driven paleoclimate/stratigraphy/macrorevolution, as well as development of geoscientific cyberinfrastructure. I hold a master's degree from the **University of Tulsa** where I was supervised by [Bryan Tapp](https://faculty.utulsa.edu/faculty/bryan-tapp/) with a focus in computational geomechanics.
